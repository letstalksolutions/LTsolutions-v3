Philosophy

What I believe

Four principles that shape every engagement, from discovery through to handover.

Orchestration over automation

The value is never in the tool itself, it is in knowing how to use it well. Automation without understanding creates fragile systems. Orchestration creates sustainable ones.

Knowledge transfer as success

A consultant who leaves you dependent has failed. Every engagement should end with your team able to maintain, extend, and troubleshoot without outside help.

Iteration over perfection

"Not quite right yet" is a feature, not a bug. Building iteratively means catching issues early and adapting to what actually works in your context.

Honest assessment

Not every problem needs a consultant. If your challenge is better solved internally or with a different approach, I will tell you. Trust is built on honesty, not on selling hours.

About Andreas

Hands-on Atlassian delivery

I'm Andreas Nyberg, an Atlassian consultant and ITSM specialist with more than 15 years of experience in workflow automation, tooling strategy, and service management transformation.

I've supported global rollouts spanning 4,000 to 5,000 seats across multiple regions, working with organisations where the challenge is never just the tooling but how teams adopt, own, and sustain it. My specialism is the Atlassian service stack: Jira Service Management, Assets (CMDB), and Opsgenie.

More than configuration, my focus is the human side of transformation: understanding how teams actually work, identifying which processes need to change, and building knowledge that stays long after the engagement ends. I believe in orchestration over automation. Tools don't solve problems; people who know how to orchestrate tools solve problems.

That philosophy shapes everything I do. If I can leave your team more capable and more confident than when I arrived, the engagement has succeeded. If you still need me afterwards, something went wrong.

Based on repeated implementation patterns across clients, we began developing internal deployment frameworks to standardise and stabilise service desk rollouts. This work has now evolved into JSM Launchpad, a deployable configuration framework for Jira Service Management.
