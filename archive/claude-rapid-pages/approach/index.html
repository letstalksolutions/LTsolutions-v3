Our Approach

A framework that builds independence

Every engagement follows a clear, practical sequence that prioritises knowledge transfer and sustainable ownership. The goal isn't dependency; it's capability.

Phase 1

Discovery and Diagnosis

Before any configuration, I need to understand where you are, where you want to be, and what's actually blocking you. This phase is about asking the right questions and listening carefully to the answers.

What happens

  • Stakeholder interviews to understand real needs
  • Current state assessment of tools and processes
  • Pain point mapping and prioritisation
  • Quick win identification
  • Success criteria definition

Deliverables

Discovery report with findings, recommendations, and prioritised roadmap.

Why this matters

Most failed implementations start with assumptions. Discovery removes guesswork. It ensures we're solving the right problems, not just the obvious ones.

Phase 2

Mapping the Path

With diagnosis complete, we define the path forward. This isn't a vague roadmap; it's a specific plan with clear scope, realistic timelines, and agreed deliverables.

What happens

  • Scope definition and boundary setting
  • Sprint planning and milestone agreement
  • Resource and dependency identification
  • Risk assessment and mitigation planning
  • Success metrics definition

Deliverables

Project plan with milestones, responsibilities, and acceptance criteria.

Why this matters

No surprises. You know exactly what you're getting, when you're getting it, and how we'll know it's done. Clear expectations prevent scope creep and misalignment.

Phase 3

Implementation and Integration

This is where the work happens. Configuration, automation, integration, testing. Iterative delivery with regular check-ins to ensure we're on track.

What happens

  • Tool configuration aligned to agreed design
  • Automation rule development and testing
  • System integration and data migration
  • User acceptance testing with stakeholders
  • Iterative refinement based on feedback

Deliverables

Configured systems, tested automations, integrated workflows, and UAT sign-off.

Why this matters

Implementation isn't just about making things work; it's about making things work for your context. Regular check-ins ensure we're building what you need, not what we assumed.

Phase 4

Knowledge Transfer and Delivery

The most important phase. Documentation, training, and handover that ensures your team can maintain and extend what we've built. You stay independent.

What happens

  • Comprehensive documentation of all configurations
  • Admin training for your technical team
  • User training for end users
  • Runbooks for common scenarios
  • Handover sessions with Q&A

Deliverables

Documentation package, trained team, and clear ownership of ongoing maintenance.

Why this matters

A consultant who leaves you dependent has failed. The goal is capability transfer, not ongoing dependency. Your team should be able to maintain, troubleshoot, and extend without calling me.

Guiding Principles

How I work

These principles guide every engagement, regardless of scope or complexity.

Orchestration over automation

Tools don't solve problems. People who know how to orchestrate tools solve problems. I focus on the human side, not just the technical configuration.

Iteration over perfection

"Not quite right yet" is a feature, not a bug. Building iteratively means we catch issues early and adapt to what actually works for your team.

Clarity over complexity

Simple solutions that your team understands beat clever solutions that only I can maintain. Complexity is the enemy of sustainability.

Documentation as deliverable

If it's not documented, it didn't happen. Every configuration, every decision, every process gets written down in language your team can follow.

Knowledge transfer as success

The measure of success isn't what I build; it's what your team can maintain after I leave. Independence is the goal.

Honest assessment

Not every problem needs a consultant. If your challenge is better solved internally or with different tools, I'll tell you. No sales pitch, just honest advice.
